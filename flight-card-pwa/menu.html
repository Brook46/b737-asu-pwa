📲 Install it on your iPad

Open it from Safari once, add it to your Home Screen, and from then on it behaves like a real app — full-screen, offline-capable, Slide-Over-ready.

  1. Open the app in Safari Visit brook46.github.io/b737-asu-pwa/flight-card-pwa/. Safari (not Chrome) — only Safari can install PWAs on iPadOS.
  2. Tap the Share button The ⬆ square-with-arrow at the top of the Safari window.
  3. Pick "Add to Home Screen" Scroll down in the share sheet — it's a row with a small grey ＋. Rename it Flight Card if you like, then tap Add.
  4. Launch from the Home Screen The blue ✈ icon now lives on your Home Screen. Tap it once to launch — the first launch caches the whole app, so it works offline forever after that.
Why not just use the Safari tab? The installed PWA gets its own window, can go into Slide Over, runs offline, survives a restart, and doesn't lose state when you switch apps. The Safari tab can't do any of those.

🪟 Use it as a floating window (Slide Over)

Keep Flight Card hovering over your OPT, Lido, or charts app — same screen, no app switch. Slide-Over is iPadOS's killer multitasking trick; the installed PWA plays nicely with it.

  1. Pin Flight Card to the Dock Long-press the ✈ icon on the Home Screen → Add to Dock. (If it's already in the Dock, skip this step.) Recently-used apps don't always show up in Slide Over — pinning makes it reliable.
  2. Open your main app first Launch the app you'll be working in full-screen — Lido, OPT, Pilots, Documents, whatever.
  3. Show the Dock Swipe up briefly from the very bottom edge of the screen — about an inch — and let go. The Dock appears across the bottom. (Swipe further and you'll switch to the App Switcher instead.)
  4. Drag Flight Card onto the screen Press and hold the ✈ Flight Card icon in the Dock, drag it up toward the middle of the screen, then release. It pops up as a narrow floating window on the right.
  5. Park it where you want it Drag the small grey handle at the top of the floating window to the left or right edge to dock it there. Swipe the handle off-screen to hide Flight Card without closing it — a thin grey edge stays visible and a single swipe brings it back.
Three-dot menu on iPadOS 16+: the trio of dots at the top of every window opens the same menu — pick the middle icon (a small rectangle to one side) for Slide Over. Tap the rightmost icon to flip back to full-screen.
Heads-up: Slide Over only works with installed PWAs — a Safari tab can't go in Slide Over. If the icon doesn't drag out of the Dock, re-check that you installed Flight Card to the Home Screen first.

📅 Connect your Google Calendar

Paste your calendar's secret iCal address once. From then on the app pulls every leg automatically, 3 hours before each departure, with no sign-in and no OAuth dance.

On a Mac or PC, grab the secret address from Google Calendar:

  1. Open Google Calendar settings Sign in at calendar.google.com, click the gear ⚙ → Settings.
  2. Pick your duty calendar In the left sidebar, click the calendar you keep your flights on (under Settings for my calendars).
  3. Find "Integrate calendar" Scroll all the way down to Integrate calendar.
  4. Copy "Secret address in iCal format" It's a long URL starting with https://calendar.google.com/calendar/ical/… and ending in basic.ics. Tap the copy icon next to it.
    ⚠ This URL is a password — anyone with it can read your calendar. Don't share it.

Then in Flight Card on the iPad:

  1. Open the share / sync sheet Tap the ▣ icon in the header.
  2. Paste into "Secret iCal URL" The field hides what you type (•••••), so make sure the paste is clean. Tap Save calendar.
  3. Tap "Sync now" Watch every leg of the month appear in the leg switcher, sorted by UTC dep.
How auto-refresh works: the app re-syncs 3 hours before each leg's departure (and on app launch if it's been more than 60 min). Manual refresh always works from the same sheet. Within the 3-hour lock window before departure, your typed crew names and flight time are sticky — sync won't overwrite them, so an evening dispatch update can't silently revert a name you fixed in the cockpit.
Keep the secret URL safe. Once it's saved the field shows dots and you can't read it back. Stash the original in 1Password / Apple Passwords so you can re-paste if you ever clear the app's data.

📋 Or paste your bulletin (manual fallback)

Your monthly bulletin is the fastest way to fill the app. One paste and every leg of the duty period lands in your leg list, sorted by UTC departure.

  1. Copy your duty bulletin Open the e-bulletin (Slip details / Cockpit format) and select-all → copy the text.
  2. Open Flight Card Tap the ✈ New Flight icon in the header.
  3. Tap "Paste roster" Paste into the box and tap Parse.
  4. Done Every leg shows up in the switcher with dep / arr / flight # / tail / schedule. The current-time leg is auto-selected; tap 🛫 any time to jump back to "now".
Single flight, no bulletin? Skip the paste — just type a flight number in the header pill (e.g. 27). If it matches a known ELY route the popup offers to create the leg with dep / arr already filled in.

📸 OPT / FMC screenshot reader OCR

Skip the typing — feed it the picture you already have.

Tap ⌖ OPT / FMC on the Takeoff performance group, pick a screenshot from Photos. The parser pulls out:

  • Tail from PROFILE EHB → normalised to 4X-EHB.
  • Flaps from the bottom output value.
  • N1 from TO2 93.2 (derate) or N1 92.5 (full thrust).
  • V1 / VR / V2 from the bottom-right block.
Powered by Tesseract.js. Cached on first use → works offline forever after that.

🧳 Per-leg state v7+

Leg 2's fuel doesn't bleed into leg 1. Ever.

Each leg owns its own data card and checklist. Switching legs swaps V-speeds, fuel, ATIS, SOB and checklist ticks with it. Crew + flight identity ride along automatically.

The 🛫 button next to the leg switcher arrows jumps to whichever leg's dep → arr window contains the current UTC.

🪪 Header — ELY flight number

Type digits (1, 27, 337) and the header auto-renders ELY###. Switching to a new number:

  • Matches a leg you already have → it switches to it.
  • Brand new → confirm popup shows route + typical block time and creates a new leg.

The ↗ next to the CTOT pill opens FlightAware for the current ELY ### so you can look up real ETD / slot.

📣 PA library EN + HE

Default set: Welcome, Go around, Emergency, Ditching, Smoke / fire, Malfunction, Delays. Hebrew on top (RTL), English below (LTR). Edit mode adds:

📐 Block · Actual · PF / PM

The Flight group grows four logbook fields: Block (scheduled), Actual (real flight time), and PF / PM roles for takeoff & landing.

  • Block — captured the first time the leg appears in your calendar. Read-only afterwards so a late dispatch tweak to flight time doesn't shift the scheduled block on your logbook.
  • Actual — empty until you fill it in or (Phase 4 coming) the GPS detector marks takeoff / landing. Manually editable so you can correct or back-fill any leg.
  • T/O and LDG — three-state pills (—, PF, PM) per leg. Tap to cycle. Used in the logbook export's event title (e.g. ELY27 TLV→VIE (PF/PM)) and in the Phase 5 analytics breakdown.

📡 GPS auto-detect + Max G landing score

The iPad's GPS marks takeoff and landing automatically; the motion sensor captures the touchdown G force. Both only run while the PWA is foregrounded and you've granted permission.

  1. Allow Geolocation + Motion Open Settings (⚙) → Sensors. Tap Request on each row. iOS asks once per origin. Decline either — the matching field (Actual flight time / Max G) just stays manually editable.
  2. Open the app before pushback Inside the leg's active window (Dep − 30 min → Arr + 30 min) the detector arms automatically. Keep Flight Card in Slide Over or front so GPS keeps ticking.
  3. Watch for the airborne toast Once groundspeed > 120 kt for 30 sec and you're climbing, the detector marks takeoff and starts capturing G.
  4. Landing score appears on touchdown After ground speed < 80 kt for 60 sec with a flat altitude, the detector marks landing and pops the score modal:
  • < 1.30 G — Good smooth touchdown
  • 1.30 – 1.60 G — OK typical
  • > 1.60 G — Poor buy the cabin coffee
Limits to know: iOS Safari only fires GPS / Motion while the tab/PWA is foregrounded. Slide Over works, but if you switch to another full-screen app, sampling pauses until you come back. The detector resumes from wherever it left off — it doesn't reset.
Manual override always wins. Actual flight time, Max G, T/O role, LDG role are all editable. Phase 4 just auto-fills them when it can.

📓 Logbook .ics export

Turn every leg you've ever flown into a calendar feed you can subscribe to. One tap from Settings.

  1. Open Settings (⚙) → Export .ics Generates a `flightcard-logbook-YYYYMMDD.ics` covering every leg in your current flight + history.
  2. iOS Share sheet appears Save to Files / AirDrop to a Mac / e-mail to yourself — whatever you like.
  3. Subscribe in Apple Calendar On the Mac, double-click the .ics → Calendar offers to import or subscribe. Choose Subscribe for a feed that updates each time you re-export.
  4. …or import into Google Calendar Settings → Import & export → Import → pick the .ics → choose a target calendar (consider making a dedicated Logbook).

Each event packs the leg's tail, crew (with nicknames if you've set them), Block, Actual, Max G, and ATIS letter into the description. Stable UIDs per leg so re-importing updates rather than duplicates.

🪂 AirDrop full-state sync

Settings sheet → Sync everything (AirDrop) exports the whole app state as JSON via the iOS Share Sheet. Import on the other device replaces local state — useful when you want every leg, every speech, and the template moved across in one shot.

Light · Dark · ASU green — cycle with the theme icon. Orientation lock is off so landscape works on iPhone; if your installed PWA doesn't rotate, remove the home-screen icon and re-Add to Home Screen so it picks up the current manifest.
